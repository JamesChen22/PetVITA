Severity Levels Definition:

1. Normal
	* Calm posture
	* Natural movement
	* Relaxed breathing
	* No distress vocalization
2. Possible Abnormal
	* Repetitive pacing
	* Unusual posture
	* Persistent staring
	* Subtle whining
	* Minor behavioral deviation
3. Needs Attention
	* Repeated barking without visible trigger
	* Scratching door repeatedly
	* Visible discomfort
	* Refusal to move
	* Signs of dehydration or hunger
4. Possible Emergency
	* Continuous distress vocalization
	* Collapse
	* Seizure-like movement
	* Labored breathing
	* Immobility with abnormal posture

Evaluation Instructions:

Use both video and audio evidence.
If audio and video conflict, prioritize visible physical condition.
Be conservative — only escalate severity if clear evidence exists.
If uncertain between two levels, choose the lower severity.
Consider duration, repetition, and intensity of behavior.
